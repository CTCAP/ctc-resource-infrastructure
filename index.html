Why
The problem
Resources exist.
They just can't be found.
Across the CTC network, affiliates in cities around the world have built strategies, tools, curriculum, and coaching materials. Most of it lives in someone's Drive — invisible to everyone else. The knowledge exists. The infrastructure to share it doesn't.
Why
The principle
Governed by the people
who built it.
Affiliates retain ownership. Nothing is shared beyond what they explicitly permit. The system is designed to serve the people it depends on.
Files stay in affiliate-controlled Drive. The system holds metadata and links only.
Permission is set at submission. Nothing becomes more visible without explicit approval.
Withdrawal is always possible. Any resource can be removed at any time through the stream coordinator.
Participation is invited, not required. The network grows through trust, not mandate.

How
Two consent processes — one sequence
Who gets approached first, and why
First · resource teams
Co-designers, not end users
Coaching, Training, and Hub City teams shape the system before affiliates see it. Their buy-in gives CTC legitimate authority in affiliate conversations.
Second · affiliates
A trust conversation, not a features tour
Affiliates own the resources. Their agreement to the permissions model is structural. Outreach flows through each stream coordinator's existing relationships.
Each stream coordinator is already in relationship with the affiliates most relevant to their stream. The bridge to affiliates runs through them.
How
Design philosophy
Designed before it is built
Five stages bring the right people in before anything is built. Timeline is process-driven, not date-driven.
01
Team alignmentOne-on-one with each stream lead — testing direction, not briefing a plan.
02
Stream working sessionsEach stream maps what it collects, who owns it, and what vocabulary it uses.
03
Affiliate outreachThrough coordinator relationships — we are designing this together before anything is built.
04
Participatory surveyAffiliates vote on category tags, permission options, and language fields.
05
Schema consolidationShared vocabulary agreed across all three streams. Build decision follows.
